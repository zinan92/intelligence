# Jade Sample Frame Notes

The jade sample frame was designed to answer a simple research question: which jade product directions are already real in the market, and which adjacent signals are credible enough to test next?

## Sample Mix

The working sample mix intentionally blended three pools:

- `core jade accounts` to establish category reality and repeated winning patterns
- `adjacent-category accounts` to surface style language that might migrate into jade
- `keyword discovery sets` to catch both known demand and emerging search language

## Why the Mix Matters

Jade research can drift quickly if the sample overweights one account type. Purely commercial accounts can hide style evolution. Purely aesthetic accounts can hide what actually sells. Pure adjacent-category research can create interesting but irrelevant noise.

The mix above keeps the study grounded in product reality while still leaving room for younger and more experimental jade directions.

## Core Selection Logic

Core jade accounts were only useful when they showed product-specific notes with visual detail. The strongest samples usually repeated one or more of the following:

- material combinations
- shape language
- styling logic
- repeatable visual systems

The sample frame also assumed image-first review. If the product could not be read visually, it stayed incomplete rather than being forced into a trend claim.

## Audience and Price Fit

The jade case study focused on the `3000-8000 RMB` band because it was specific enough to evaluate commercial plausibility without collapsing into luxury-only analysis. That range also made it easier to compare jade against adjacent jewelry categories that already use clearer style language.
