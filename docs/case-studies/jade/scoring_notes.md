# Jade Scoring Notes

The jade study used scoring as a judgment layer, not as a replacement for upstream engagement metrics.

## Buckets

Each sample was judged across four buckets:

- image strength
- interaction strength
- comment signal strength
- cross-category resonance

## What the Jade Case Proved

The strongest jade signals were not the posts with the largest raw numbers. They were the posts where the product looked distinctive, the audience reacted for a specific reason, and the same visual language appeared again in nearby accounts or adjacent categories.

That is why image strength and cross-category resonance matter so much in this case study. Engagement alone can surface noise. The stronger signal is whether the design language feels repeatable.

## Practical Rule

For jade, the scoring layer worked best when it asked three questions:

- does the product look memorable?
- does the audience react with concrete interest?
- does the direction show up beyond one isolated note?

If the answer was only yes to the first question, the direction stayed in watchlist territory. If the answer was yes across all three, the direction became a candidate for build or test work.
